Collaborative dereverberation

Sanna Wager (Ph.D. candidate, Indiana University SoIC), Minje Kim (Indiana University ISE)

Overview

This page contains sample audio results related to our collaborative-dereverberation paper. We extend the Nonnegative-Tensor-Tactorization (NTF) multi-channel dereverberation model proposed by [1]—where channel-specific speech dereveberation tasks are linked by jointly estimating the speech source across channels—by using Nonnegative Matrix Factorization (NMF) along with sparsity and total variation regularization to add prior knowledge about dry speech to the source reconstruction.

We ran the experiment on three different room types, with reverberation decay times (T60), respectively, of 0.6, 1.2, and 1.6 seconds. Speech recordings with longer decay times are challenging cases for applications such as automatic speech recognition. The input to the program consists of monophonic recordings of four sensors placed in different parts of the room. The output consists of a single dry-signal estimate. This page presents example audio results for one source/sensor configuration per room along with the corresponding average STOI and SNR values. We show the results of three different dereverberation models: the baseline (NTF) model [1] adapted to an objective function using KL-divergence instead of Euclidean distance, the NTF model we extended with an NMF speech prior, and our proposed model of NTF with an NMF speech prior and regularization.
